About

Ryan has over ten years of experience as a professional Graphic Designer. A few years ago, he added Web Design and Development to his resume and holds a Web Design Specialist professional certificate. Currently, Ryan is a Software Engineer II – Front End Developer for Asurion building claims web applications for some of the largest wireless carriers in the world. Previously, he worked as a Front End Developer and Designer for Learning Tree International and as a Federal Contractor for the US Department of Agriculture, Department of Justice, and the Food and Drug Administration.

As a Graphics Designer, he has designed Congressional Reports for the US Department of Justice, IAS Information Brochure for the USDA, PSD department, and News Communications for the FDA CFSAN office.

Mr. Eagle also has a great deal of experience with the Virginia Wine industry providing services for Barrel Oak Winery, Vint Hill Craft Winery, Pearmund Cellars, Philip Carter Winery, The Winery at La Grange, Fox Meadow Winery, and Gray Ghost Winery. His talents have also been contributed overseas providing design services for Azerbaijan Airlines corporate executives.

Procurement Forecasting

The USDA’s OSDBU had a legacy procurement forecasting system that was built on a floppy drive and mailed around to the different sub-agencies to collect procurement information needs. Not only was this a very dated method, but it was very susceptible to risk of being corrupted. DRT Strategies was approached to assemble a small team to develop a system that was scalable, low risk, and was expandable. We developed a web portal that contractor’s access and preform their own searches based on the services they provide. There is also a user login for the sub-agencies to upload and populate their procurement needs. Once the administrator approves the procurement requests, the data is then pushed to the procurement database for the contractors to access. The Procurement Forecasting website was fully responsive so that government employees working in the field could login and submit their procurement needs from a smartphone or tablet.

  • Client: Office of Small & Disadvantaged Business Utilization
  • Date: June - September 2014
  • Service: Web Development & UI Design

IAS

The USDA’s Procurement Systems Division has an informational website that acts like a gateway to their online acquisition portals. Ryan was hired for basic content management and simple improvements to the site based on the scope of the contract. After identifying the limitations and the dated development structure, he decided to build a whole new, modern website that utilized the real-estate of the site more effectively to convey information to the users more effectively. An interactive Calendar was also developed to help users see upcoming training opportunities and where the training would be held. A feature that was being developed but was never finished during the contract duration was the ability for users to go to the website and submit a ticket for issues they may have experienced while submitting an acquisition request. This was meant to help decrease the backlog of the help desk and streamline the process in which help desk personnel would take to resolve tickets.

  • Client: Procurement Systems Dvision
  • Date: October 2013 - September 2014
  • Service: Web Design, Web Development, & Content Management
